# Skills Mismatch in Digital Transformation: A Capability-Based View

**Authors:** Hoffmann, T., Singh, A., & Carvalho, M.
**Year:** 2024
**Journal:** MIS Quarterly, 48(2), 891-918

## Abstract

Digital transformation initiatives frequently fail not from a lack of technology but from a misalignment between organisational capabilities and the demands of new digital tools. Using a capability-based view, we surveyed 412 managers across four sectors (banking, healthcare, retail, professional services) and conducted 26 follow-up interviews. We find that what distinguishes successful adopters is not technical skill depth, but **managerial judgement skills**: the ability to scope use cases, evaluate AI outputs, and recognise when to override automated recommendations. We propose a framework for "judgement-augmented automation".

## Research Question

Which managerial capabilities most strongly differentiate successful from unsuccessful digital transformation outcomes?

## Method

- **Design:** Cross-sectional survey + follow-up interviews
- **Quantitative:** n=412 managers, 4 sectors
- **Qualitative:** 26 follow-up interviews, 60-90 min
- **Analysis:** Structural equation modelling; framework analysis

## Key Findings

1. Technical skill depth has weak correlation with transformation success (r=0.18).
2. Judgement skills — scope, evaluate, override — have strong correlation (r=0.61).
3. Most surveyed firms invest 8× more in technical training than judgement training.
4. Younger managers report higher confidence in AI use but lower accuracy in identifying flawed outputs.

## Stated Limitations

- Self-report bias in survey instruments.
- "Success" is operationally defined as self-rated; objective performance metrics were unavailable.
- Sector mix excludes manufacturing and public sector, both of which face distinct transformation dynamics.
