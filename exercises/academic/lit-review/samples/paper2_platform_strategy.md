# Platform Strategy and Generative AI: Rethinking Two-Sided Markets

**Authors:** Almeida, P. & Yamamoto, K.
**Year:** 2023
**Journal:** Strategic Management Journal, 44(11), 2789-2815

## Abstract

We examine how the introduction of generative AI capabilities reshapes platform strategy in two-sided markets. Drawing on a longitudinal case study of three digital platforms (one e-commerce, one freelance services, one educational content) between 2021 and 2023, we show that generative AI does not merely augment existing platform features — it reconfigures the producer/consumer boundary. Platforms increasingly position themselves as **co-producers** rather than neutral intermediaries, raising new questions about value capture, content liability, and platform governance.

## Research Question

How does generative AI integration alter the strategic positioning and value capture mechanisms of two-sided platforms?

## Method

- **Design:** Longitudinal multi-case study
- **Cases:** 3 platforms across distinct verticals
- **Data:** 47 interviews, 280+ internal documents, public filings 2021-2023
- **Analysis:** Cross-case pattern matching, inductive theory building

## Key Findings

1. Generative AI shifts platforms from "matchmakers" to "co-producers" — they now actively generate content rather than only routing it.
2. This co-producer role creates new liability exposure, especially around copyright and accuracy.
3. Value capture migrates upstream: platforms charge for generation, not just transactions.
4. Producer-side users (sellers, freelancers, creators) report mixed sentiment — 58% see AI as opportunity, 42% as threat.

## Stated Limitations

- Small case sample (n=3) limits generalisability.
- Cases are all UK/EU based; US and Asian dynamics may differ.
- Study period predates major regulatory responses (EU AI Act came into force after data collection).
