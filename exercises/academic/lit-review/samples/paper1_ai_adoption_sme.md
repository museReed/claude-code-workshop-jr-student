# AI Adoption in UK Small and Medium Enterprises: A Mixed-Methods Study

**Authors:** Chen, L., Mitchell, R., & Okafor, J.
**Year:** 2024
**Journal:** Journal of Small Business Management, 62(3), 412-438

## Abstract

This study investigates the determinants of artificial intelligence (AI) adoption among UK small and medium enterprises (SMEs). Using a mixed-methods design combining a survey of 287 SME owners and 18 semi-structured interviews, we identify three primary drivers: perceived competitive pressure, availability of pre-trained models, and prior experience with cloud computing services. Cost was reported as a significant barrier in only 34% of cases, contradicting earlier studies. We argue that the rise of API-based AI services has fundamentally restructured the adoption decision, shifting it from a capital expenditure question to an operational one.

## Research Question

What factors most strongly predict AI tool adoption in UK SMEs in the post-LLM era (2022 onwards)?

## Method

- **Design:** Sequential explanatory mixed methods
- **Quantitative:** Online survey, n=287, stratified by sector and headcount
- **Qualitative:** 18 semi-structured interviews, 45-60 min each
- **Analysis:** Logistic regression (quant); thematic analysis (qual)

## Key Findings

1. Competitive pressure is the strongest predictor (β=0.42, p<0.001).
2. SMEs with prior cloud adoption are 3.2× more likely to adopt AI within 12 months.
3. Cost-as-barrier narrative is outdated; only 34% cited it as primary obstacle.
4. Sector matters less than expected — adoption rates were similar across services, retail, and manufacturing.

## Stated Limitations

- Cross-sectional design cannot establish causal direction between cloud and AI adoption.
- Sample over-represents London and South East England (61%).
- Survey was conducted before the GPT-4 release, which may have shifted the landscape.
