# My Housing Criteria

## Background

I'm starting MSc Technology Management at KCL (Strand campus) in September. Coming from overseas, never lived in London. Budget is tight but I want somewhere I can actually study and sleep.

## Budget

- **Hard ceiling:** £1,400/month all-in (rent + bills + council tax)
- **Preferred:** £1,100-1,250
- **One-off costs I can afford:** deposit up to 5 weeks rent (~£1,500), first month upfront. NOT more.

## Non-negotiables (deal-breakers)

1. Commute to KCL Strand must be **under 45 minutes door-to-door** by tube/walking.
2. Must have a **proper desk** in the room (or space for one).
3. Must have **window in the bedroom** (no internal rooms).
4. Internet included or trivially set up (no contracts I can't get out of in 12 months).
5. No guarantor requirements that need a UK-based homeowner (I don't have one).
6. Lease length between 9-12 months — I'm here for one academic year.

## Would-be-nice

- Walking distance to a supermarket (Sainsbury / Tesco / Aldi)
- Near a park
- Roommates who are also students or young professionals (not 5 random strangers)
- Washing machine in the flat (not communal laundrette)
- Zone 1 or 2 preferred but Zone 3 OK if commute is fast

## Strong preference: bills included

I don't want to deal with setting up British Gas / Thames Water / council tax registration as my first month in the UK. If bills are included even at a slight premium, that's worth it.

## Red flags I'm wary of

- Listings that ask for **6 months rent upfront** (heard this is a scam pattern targeting internationals)
- Anyone refusing video call viewings — I'll be doing this remotely
- Properties without proper addresses ("near Vauxhall area" without postcode)
- People asking for deposit via bank transfer to a personal account before lease is signed
