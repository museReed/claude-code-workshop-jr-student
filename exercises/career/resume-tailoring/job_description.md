## About Lattice Pay

We're a London-based fintech building the payments infrastructure for the next generation of marketplace and platform businesses. We process £2bn+ in transactions annually for 800+ business customers across the UK and EU. We're backed by leading European VCs and growing fast — 3× in the last 18 months.

Our culture: small teams, ship fast, no political games, lots of ownership early.

---

## The Role

Our **Graduate Product Manager Programme** is a 24-month rotational track designed for people who want to learn product management at speed in a high-growth fintech. You'll rotate across three teams (8 months each):

1. **Core payments** — the engine that powers transactions
2. **Merchant tools** — dashboards and APIs our customers use
3. **Risk & compliance** — fraud detection, KYC, regulatory reporting

You'll work alongside senior PMs, engineers, designers, and customers. By the end of the programme, you'll have shipped multiple features end-to-end and be ready for a Product Manager role.

## What we're looking for

### Required

- Bachelor's degree (any discipline) with strong academic record
- A second qualification, work experience, or extensive side projects that show you can **build something real** — not just write about it
- Comfort with **structured problem-solving**: framing ambiguous problems, decomposing them, prioritising
- **Strong written communication** — you can take a messy meeting and turn it into a clear decision document
- **Quantitative comfort** — you don't need to be an engineer, but you must be able to read a SQL query, interrogate a dashboard, and challenge a number that looks wrong
- Genuine curiosity about how businesses work — fintech specifically is a plus but not required
- The right to work in the UK (we sponsor Graduate visas; we cannot sponsor full Skilled Worker visas for this role)

### Preferred

- Background in engineering, computer science, economics, business, or related quantitative discipline
- Experience shipping something — a project, a side-business, a non-profit, an internal tool — that real users used
- Exposure to startups, hackathons, or entrepreneurship
- Familiarity with at least one of: SQL, Python, Figma, basic analytics tools
- Experience working in cross-functional teams (engineers + designers + business)
- Languages beyond English — we operate across multiple EU markets

### What success looks like in year 1

- By month 3, you've shipped your first small feature end-to-end
- By month 6, you're running your own discovery interviews with customers
- By month 12, you've led a substantial project that closed a measurable gap (revenue, retention, or risk)
